French Toast

Description

French toast is a classic breakfast dish that is easy to make and always a hit with the whole family. It involves dipping slices of bread in a mixture of beaten eggs and milk, then cooking them in a skillet or on a griddle until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside. The bread is then typically served with a variety of toppings such as maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruit, or whipped cream. French toast is a delicious and satisfying way to start the day, and it can be easily customized to suit your personal taste preferences. Whether you prefer your French toast sweet or savory, with a hint of cinnamon or a sprinkle of vanilla, this versatile recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household.

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  2. Lightly butter a griddle and heat over medium-high heat.
  3. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side. Serve hot.
