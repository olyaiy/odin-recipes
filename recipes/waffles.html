Waffles

Description

Waffles are a beloved breakfast treat that is perfect for any occasion. They are made by mixing flour, sugar, baking powder, and eggs together to create a batter, which is then poured into a waffle iron and cooked until it is golden brown and crispy. The best part about waffles is that they can be customized to suit your personal preferences – you can add fruit, chocolate chips, nuts, or other ingredients to the batter to give them extra flavor and texture. Waffles are traditionally served with butter and syrup, but you can also top them with fresh fruit, whipped cream, or any other toppings that you like. Whether you prefer your waffles sweet or savory, they are sure to be a hit with the whole family. So why not give them a try and see for yourself just how delicious they can be?

  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ¾ cups milk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • nonstick cooking spray

Steps

  1. Preheat a waffle iron according to manufacturer's instructions
  2. Whisk eggs in a large bowl until light and fluffy. Add flour, milk, and vegetable oil and mix to combine. Whisk in sugar, then mix in baking powder, salt, and vanilla just until smooth, being careful not to overmix.
  3. Spray the preheated waffle iron with nonstick spray. Pour batter onto the hot waffle iron and cook until golden brown and the iron stops steaming, 3 to 5 minutes.
